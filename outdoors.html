South Africa

This trip was for my eldest sister. She graduated high school in 2014. It was to celebrate her graduation. We stayed for about 3 weeks traveling all around. We got a bus for the first week with a fun group of people where we did all sorts of activities such as zip lining, sand boarding and exploring caves. We took a leisurely pace for the last part with doing Airbnbs. We occupied ourselves with other youthful activities such as Table Mountain.

Hawaii

This trip was for my high school graduation. This was my second time coming here after my youngest sister's high school graduation. We stayed for about 3 weeks with visiting 3 different islands: Kauai, The Big Island and Maui. We did all sorts of outside activities from doing a boat ride that went to an active volcano to a helicopter tour. The best part was the boat tour as you got to see actual lava. The steam surrounding the boat was fun to experience as well.

California

My mother and I visited my eldest sister a few weeks before her wedding. It was to make sure everything was in place for her special day. We explored several parks in the area. My best friend goes to college here. We were able to connect up with him on the one of the days. Our day was spent hiking up a steep, rockey mountain catching up on what we have been doing.

California

A few weeks later, it was time for my sister's special day. She decided to have a small wedding with close family. She got married on a Friday but before then, we continued exploring California. We would go to the bay and continued our hiking sessions. One hike was very steep going down at the end of the trail. It's surprising (but a blessing) that no one fell with how steep it was.

Portugal

A few months later, we took a family vacation instead of doing the usual. Our usual for Christmas is visiting our family in Rochester but we did that in advance because we wanted to do something different for the holidays. We decided to go to two different countries: the other being Spain. Our family was sadly not able to meet up together due to the overarching concerns about COVID but we were able to split it up between the countries. Portugal was easy due to staying in one spot. We mainly explored the capital and a few churches. We also took a cooking class which was unique but fun.

Spain

When the new year came, we went to Spain. My eldest and her husband had to go back to work but my youngest and her fiancee were able to join for this portion. This was an interesting week. We did a city each day which was about 2-3 hours away. We did all of southern Spain with exploring the cities, churches and castles.

Sedona

This was my final spring break of college. Arizona is hot in the summer, but in winter, it's amazing. We mainly hiked all around the Sedona area like Cathedral Rock. It was very convient with the Airbnb we got. The trails would be about 5-10 minutes away. We would get up early in the morning and hike for about 4-5 hours before going back to relax. We did some other activities such as a Pink Jeep Tour, which was a blast with the road, but it was mainly hiking.

Alaska

This was for my college graduation. We went all around Alaska. The time limit we had was 3 weeks. With the amount of cities we wanted to do, we gave ourselves about 3 days per city. Our first destination after a long flight was Ancorage. We spent the night in a bed and breakfast to recharge. Then we went to several different places such as Fairbanks and Whittier. During those places, we would experience the breathtaking glaciers. We would also hike on the actual glacier along with the deep, snowy trails. Stranger Things 4 came out during this time so we gave ourselves a relax day in Fairbanks. It lined up perfectly due to being Memorial day.

Nigara Falls

I have been here before but that was back in middle school. This recent trip was to visit my youngest sister and help prepare for her wedding. We did not do many hiking trails like for my oldest, but we did historic places instead when we were preparing for her wedding. Nigara Falls is always a magnificant place to experience with its structure. The activities such as Maid of the Mist and Cave of the Winds are always fun. One thing that was new was going over to the Canada side. Unfortunately, the view was not as spectacular as it should be due to the cloudy day but that just gives a reason to go back for next time.

Syracuse, New York

My youngest sister's wedding came full circle. We lived in Syracuse for a brief period but I do not remember much of it due to being very young at the time. I attended kindergarten here. My sister's wedding was at a church where she got her confirmation name. The church was beautiful and it was very different compared to my oldest sister's. This one included friends and family, both close and cousins. After being a party animal on the dance floor, we went apple picking. The car was loaded with leftovers from the donuts that was served at the wedding to the apples we picked at the farm.

Early life

My family is big into sports. My parents would get my sisters and I into several different sports like baseball, basketball and soccer. None of them stuck with me because I was not a fan of running around as a kid. I did not like tennis either with how difficult it was to hit the ball and get a serve in the correct spot on the field.

Middle School

The activities I did like was dodgeball and surpringly flag football. Tennis was surpringly the one spot that stayed. Dodgeball and flag football were more school events. What made me like tennis was my friends from school were into it as well. That made getting things wrong more bearable. As time went on, I would be able to hit the ball very well and genuinely enjoy tennis for what it was. Sadly, my middle school did not have a tennis team but my high school and I could not wait to graduate middle school once I heard about it.

September 2013

The moment September 2013 came around, I was ready to sign up for the team. I found out that there two different levels: JV and varsity. Sadly, the boys tennis does not play until spring as the women's team play during the fall. The wait sucked but it was worth it when spring came around.

March 2015

I was sadly not able to be on the varisity team but I got good enough to where I was able to make during my junior year of high school. I was 3rd doubles and 5th singles. Not the best but I have put in the effort to make it on the varsity team. I was able to maintain the spot during senior year for doubles but made it to 4th singles.

Graduation

During my final year of high school, I had maintained my doubles and singles positions. Once I graduated, I got a certificate. When the time came to look for colleges, I made sure there was a tennis team so I could continue to improve my skills. Fun fact: my sisters played tennis through high school but did not continue playing in college like I did.

August 2018

College was interesting. After being told that men's tennis was only in spring during high school, I was happily shocked to find out that it happened during both fall and spring. It was coed but there was enough courts for us to practice. It was still a different ball game however, as the players in college was a lot more experience than in high school. I was lucky to make it on the team. I was 7th singles which is a substitute if any of the 6 players could not make the match but that was better than nothing.

2020

This is the COVID year. Due to COVID, we were sadly not able to continue sport activities. Even when we were able to go back class, the cases started to surge after a couple of weeks so they decided to suspend sport activities. After a couple months, the high cases thankfully went down but we lost the fall season. We tried to make up for it as much as we could during spring.

March 2022

This was final semester at college. In order to keep up with my grades, I sadly had to miss a few practices. However, this semester was light so I was able to make it to most of them. I was also able to make it 4th singles and 2nd doubles with my best friend as my partner. An interesting event that happened was that our bus broke down during April. The weirder part was that there was a snow storm. Thankfully, it was just outside of our college so we only had a short walk but that was certainly a memorable team experience as it happened at midnight. Once I graduated college, I received a similar certificate like the one I got back in high school.

Post College

After college, I would continue playing. I have not done any major leagues but I have played against my mother. She was a tennis player and is very good. We do not play any official matches but we work on strokes and serves when we can.
